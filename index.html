// 01 — Methodology

How BioSentinel Works

Six data dimensions. Three years of context. One AI engine that sees what single-visit checkups miss.

STEP_01 🩺
Continuous Data Ingestion

Every 3 months, a full-body checkup is recorded: 200+ lab biomarkers, vitals, medications, diagnoses, and diet plan. Over 3 years, this builds a rich longitudinal health timeline.

FHIR R4 Compatible
STEP_02 ⚙️
Temporal Feature Engineering

BioSentinel extracts 1,500+ temporal features: biomarker trends, slopes, variance, acceleration, cross-marker correlations, and medication-disease interaction signals across multiple time windows.

AI Processing
STEP_03 🧠
AI Risk Prediction

The BioSentinel Transformer and CancerRiskNet ensemble analyze the full health trajectory. Each of 50+ disease modules produces a calibrated risk score with SHAP-based explanations.

Explainable AI
STEP_04 🔍
Anomaly Detection

Beyond risk scores, BioSentinel continuously monitors biomarker trajectories using CUSUM change-point detection and Isolation Forest algorithms to flag subtle anomalies in real time.

Real-Time Alerts
STEP_05 ⚠️
Clinical Alerting

Multi-tier risk alerts (Green → Red) are dispatched to clinicians and patients with specific, actionable recommendations — which specialist to see, which tests to run, which lifestyle changes to make.

Clinician Dashboard
STEP_06 📈
Continuous Learning

As outcomes are recorded, models improve via feedback loops. Federated learning (Phase 3) will allow models to train across institutions without centralizing sensitive patient data.

Federated (Planned)

// 03 — Scientific Foundation

Peer-Reviewed Research Backing

BioSentinel's approach is grounded in published, validated science. These aren't theoretical claims — they've been tested on millions of real patients.

Nature Medicine · 2023
Deep Learning Predicts Pancreatic Cancer from Disease Trajectories
AI models trained on 6 million patient records in Denmark and 3 million US records predict pancreatic cancer occurrence up to 36 months in advance by learning from sequences of prior disease codes.
AUROC 0.88
BMC Med Research Methodology · 2025
AI on Longitudinal EHR Data for Cancer Prediction: Scoping Review
Comprehensive review of 33 studies across 6 major databases confirms that AI models exploiting the full longitudinal depth of electronic health records significantly outperform cross-sectional models across multiple cancer types.
33 STUDIES
Google / NIH · End-to-End Pipeline
AI Lung Nodule Detection Outperforms Radiologists
A 3D CNN trained on 42,290 CT scans from the National Lung Screening Trial achieves AUC 95.5% at external validation and outperforms average radiologist performance on malignancy risk prediction tasks.
AUC 95.5%
Cancer Medicine · 2025 · Systematic Review
ML Models for Early Cancer Diagnosis in Symptomatic Patients
Systematic review of studies from 2014–2024 demonstrates that ML algorithms integrating clinical symptoms, genetic data, lifestyle and biomarker risk factors achieve high diagnostic accuracy for multi-cancer risk stratification.
