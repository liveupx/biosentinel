# BioSentinel Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We as contributors and maintainers pledge to make participation in BioSentinel a respectful, harassment-free experience for everyone — regardless of age, disability, ethnicity, gender identity, level of experience, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity.

This project handles health data concepts and is used in clinical contexts. We hold ourselves to a higher standard of respect and accuracy than many open-source projects.

## Our Standards

**Positive behaviour:**
- Using welcoming and inclusive language
- Respectful disagreement on technical or clinical questions
- Citing evidence when making clinical claims
- Accepting constructive criticism gracefully
- Prioritising the safety of potential patients who may be affected by this software

**Unacceptable behaviour:**
- Harassment, insults, or derogatory comments
- Publishing others' private information without permission
- Making unfounded clinical claims about the software's capabilities
- Deliberately introducing vulnerabilities into healthcare software
- Any other conduct that could reasonably be considered unprofessional

## Medical Ethics

Because BioSentinel is used in healthcare contexts, contributors must:

- Never overstate the clinical validity of the models
- Always include appropriate medical disclaimers
- Report any discovered issues that could lead to patient harm immediately and privately
- Not submit changes that remove safety disclaimers or warnings

## Enforcement

Instances of unacceptable behaviour may be reported to [biosentinel@liveupx.com](mailto:biosentinel@liveupx.com). All complaints will be reviewed and investigated. The project maintainer will determine appropriate response, up to and including permanent exclusion from the project.
